## Design Context

### Users
Church tech operators, worship leaders, and AV volunteers running live services. Verses display on projectors/screens (1920x1080) in auditoriums ranging from 50-seat chapels to 5000-seat arenas. The congregation reads these at distance during worship, prayer, and teaching. Legibility at distance is non-negotiable.

### Brand Personality
Rhema is a tool for worship, not a tech product. The broadcast output should feel like it belongs in the service — never calling attention to itself as "software." Three registers coexist across the theme library:
- **Refined + purposeful**: Like a well-designed hymnal — every detail intentional
- **Bold + expressive**: Like a worship concert — confident, emotional, makes a statement
- **Warm + inviting**: Like a living room Bible study — approachable, comforting, human

### Aesthetic Direction
Each built-in theme must deliver a distinct emotional identity. No two should feel interchangeable. The range spans: sacred reverence, contemporary warmth, cinematic awe, quiet confidence.

**Anti-references (hard avoid):**
- PowerPoint clip art era (cheesy gradients, Word Art vibes, 2005 church projection)
- Generic SaaS / startup (flat minimal corporate — this is worship, not a dashboard)
- Overwrought / busy (excessive decoration, drop shadows — the verse is the star)
- AI slop tells (cyan-on-dark, purple-to-blue gradients, neon accents, glassmorphism)

### Design Principles
1. **The verse is the star** — design serves the text, never competes with it
2. **Distance legibility** — every choice must work at 30+ feet on a projector
3. **Emotional specificity** — each theme commits fully to one mood, no hedging
4. **No decoration for decoration's sake** — if it doesn't serve the verse, remove it
5. **Fonts earn their place** — every typeface chosen for its specific character, not defaults
